About the CCA Conference

Welcome to the Climate Change Awareness Conference 2020 (CCAC 2020) - to be held 28 May, 2020 in Rockfeller Center in New York, USA. The event is organized by Global Nest - an international association of scientists, technologists, engineers and other interested groups involved in all scientific and technological aspects of the environment, as well as in the application of methods aimed at sustainable development.

The conference will feature speakers representing both academia and industry who are experts in Climatology and the environmental issues. Thus, CCAC 2020 unites diverse researches and case studies from all over the world. It also offers many networking and publishing opportunities.

What We Will Speak About

Climate change impacts

On biodiversity conservation and natural resource management. Hundreds of animals and plants are already extinct. What will be next?

Climate prediction

Modeling and detection of recent and future climate change. What is already implemented, what technologies are still being developed.

Long-term climate monitoring

Climate change is one of the main challenges today. To predict the future of our planet, we need to conduct long-term climate monitoring.

Renewable energy

In 2010, 16.7% of global energy consumption came from renewable sources. In 2015, this indicator amounted to 19.3%. But it's too slow to stop the dramatical changes.

Climate change impacts

Countries all over the world should combat climate change and step up the activities needed to achieve sustainable low-carbon development.

Climate change impacts

Everyone should have physical and economic access to quality food. What will happen to the food security when the climate changes critically?

Schedule

We hope that the upcoming conference will bring together industries representatives, environmental activists, scientists and engineers to discuss the dangers of climate changes.

10:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Emma Smith

Opening speech

Emma Smith was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize this year. She held her first single picket last August at the Swedish parliament. As a special guest of CCAC, she will open our conference and tell about the impact of eco activism.

10:45 AM - 2:15 PM

Luise Gibson

Time to make real decisions

The next 2-3 years are a decisive period during which a whole series of political and investment decisions will be made that will determine the future for the next 10-15 years.

2:30 PM - 3:15 PM

Gabriel Khu

The issue of climate refugees

Every year, 20 million people are forced to leave their homes due to climate disasters. The threat of such relocations is primarily in poor countries. Climate crisis kills people, forces families to leave their homes.

3:30 PM - 5:30 PM

Calvin Tissen

Pros and cons of the winD energy

Wind energy is not a constant. With a traditional power plant, you can maintain a constant power output, but the amount of wind power depends on weather conditions. This means that you cannot fully rely on the wind.
